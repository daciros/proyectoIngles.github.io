CRISTO REDENTOR RIO DE JANEIRO (BRAZIL)




The idea of creating a gigantic image of Christ,
as a meeting point on pilgrimages and religious celebrations, dates back to the
late nineteenth century. The suggestion was brought to the knowledge of
the Princess Isabel of Brazil,
who gave the first official support to make such a great proposal, however, the
project would begin to take shape in 1921 on the occasion of celebrating the
centenary of independence. 

The choice of the place where this famous image was placed was in charge of the Catholic circle of Rio de Janeiro. The Corcovado mountain had as competitors the Pão Of Açúcar and to Mount San Antonio, but He was elected because he was the highest of the three.  
In 1923, through a contest, the engineer's
project was chosen Heitor Da Silva Costa, with plastic artist design Carlos Oswald And projected by the French architect Paul Landowsky, who came from Europe especially for this work.


Before reaching its present form, the image of
Christ the Redeemer had several modifications. The approved project of Heitor
Da Silva consisted of the image of Jesus
holding a cross in his left hand and a globe on the other hand That,
according to Princess Isabel, were the most important material attributes in
the vision of the engineer. 

To achieve the perfection of the current image
it was necessary to adopt the grid technique. With the body standing and arms
extended the construction of the monument began; Thus, the face, directed a
little downward and left, was also strategically planned to give the appearance
of being sheltering the city.

At last On October 12th, 1931 was inaugurated The Monument of Christ
the Redeemer, which years later was consecrated as a symbol of Brazilian
tourism and, today, is one of the 7 new wonders of the modern world.





AMERICAN HISTORY
MUSEUM


Since its founding in 1869, the Museum of Natural History of new York has
headed all the Rankings of the best science museums in the world. His exhibition makes A review To the history of Earth and man.


The National Museum of American History opened to the public in January 1964 as the Museum of History and Technology. It was the sixth Smithsonian building on the National Mall in Washington, D.C. Since then, some 4 million visitors a year have passed through the doors to enjoy the museum’s exhibitions, public programs, educational activities, collections, and research facilities. Millions more make virtual visits to the museum’s website.


On June 28, 1955, President Dwight D. Eisenhower signed the bill authorizing $36 million for the museum. Groundbreaking took place on August 22, 1958. The architects were McKim, Mead and White (succeeded in 1961 by Steinman, Cain and White). Mills, Petticord and Mills were associate and supervising architects and engineers. The museum’s area is approximately 750,000 square feet, including a basement, three main exhibition levels, two office-collection levels, and a mechanical penthouse on the roof.

The building’s location on the National Mall qualifies it as a National Historic Landmark. Its status as one of the last structures designed by the renowned architectural firm of McKim, Mead and White, along with its location, makes it eligible for listing on the National Register of Historic Places.

In 1980, the museum’s name was changed to the National Museum of American History to better represent its basic mission—the collection, care, and study of objects that reflect the experience of the American people.


In 2008, the museum completed a two-year, $85 million renovation of the building’s center core, dramatically transforming the building’s architectural appeal while reorganizing and renewing the presentation of its extensive collections. The renovation project focused on three areas: architectural enhancements to the center core, including a grand staircase and a skylight; construction of a new Star-Spangled Banner gallery, and updates to the 44-year-old building’s infrastructure.

Currently, the transformation of the museum is continuing with a major project to renew the building’s 120,000-square-foot west exhibition wing.

MACHU PICHU





Archaeologists estimate that it was built during the fifteenth century of
our era by the Incas, but its function remains a mystery. It is also known that
it was populated by a large number of inhabitants, but only by nobles, priests
and "Aqllas"(Virgins of the Sun). There was also a population of
peasants working the Fields But they didn't live inside the citadel.

The city is divided into 3 areas: 2 populated areas and the agricultural
sector, which is a vast system of terraces and irrigation canals. The urban
sector was divided into two neighborhoods, in one of them are the most important
temples such as the sun, as well as the Royal Chamber.




In the other neighborhood are located the houses of the nobles and the
convent of the "Virgins of the Sun." Between the two neighborhoods
there is a huge esplanade as a square. The surroundings are impressive, The
Citadel is built on top of a hill surrounded by the Urubamba River and a
mountainous chain, it looks like the center of a ring of mountains.

Perhaps the greatest attraction of Machu Picchu is the level of
technological development achieved by its builders in the field of architecture
and work in stone. The joints of the stones in some of the environments are so
narrow that not a pin could be inserted. The most significant stone is the
"Intihuatana"or solar calendar, which allowed the Incas to know
precisely the seasons and the climate throughout the year. However, many find
in Machu Picchu more than history and ancient technologies, but also an energy
and peace that surrounds everyone who arrives and visits such a mysterious place.


History of Machupicchu.

The Machu Picchu and Huayna Picchu Mountains form part of the orographic
formation known as Batholith in Vilcabamba which is located on the left bank of
the Urubamba Canyon which was known as the Quebrada de Picchu.

The story tells that in the Quebrada de Picchu they inhabited mountain
towns from the regions of Vilcabamba and the Sacred Valley.

In the period of the middle horizon there is a demographic explosion by
groups not historically documented but possibly linked to the ethnic TAMPU of
Urubamba and it is believed that these peoples could be part of the federation
who were rivals of the First Incas of Cusco.

Machu Picchu during the Inca period (1438-1534)

In 1440 when Picchu was conquered by Pachacuteq The first Inca emperor, it
was this one who in 1450 command to build an urban complex with civil and
religious constructions of great luxury as refuge of the most select of the
Inca aristocracy, a luxurious and well cared mausoleum to receive the remains
of the monarch Pachacutec. However, some of its best constructions and the
evident ceremonial character would prove that it was used as a religious
sanctuary.

The fortress was located on the eastern slope of the Cordillera del
Vilcanota, a visit to the heart of the Inca Empire and the Andean and colonial
culture. With the magnificent "Lost City of the Incas", Machu Picchu
about 80 km from Cusco, the capital of Tahuantinsuyo. Its strategic
geographical location was chosen with admirable success. Surrounded by deep
cliffs and away from the sight of strangers by a tangled jungle, the citadel of
Machu Picchu possessed the quality of having a single and narrow entrance,
which allowed, in the event of a surprise attack, to be defended by very few
warriors.

It is believed that the population in Machu Picchu was between 300 and 1000
inhabitants belonging to the Panaca Of Pachacuteq, it was an unknown place for
the inferior castes and their forbidden routes for anyone who did not form part
of the small circle of the Inca.

Within the valleys that formed the valley region are the administrative
centers of Patallacta And Quentemarca Which favored Machu Picchu by its
agricultural sectors and thus provided food to the population.

When you die Pachacuteq All their properties were passed to be managed by
their Panaca And began a new government of the Inca Túpac Yupanqui (1470-1493)
and Huayna Capac (1493-1529).

History of Machu Picchu between the Colony and
the Republic (XVII-XIX)

After the fall of Vilcabamba in 1572, Machu Picchu remained within the
jurisdiction of several colonial haciendas that changed owners during
Republican times since 1821; It became a remote place away from the new roads
and economic axes of Peru.
