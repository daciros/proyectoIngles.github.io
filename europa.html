ROME



The origin of Rome is something still unknown.
The various theories are based on the information of the ancient authors and on
the different types of Archaeological finds Found in Rome.

For these reasons, this initial stage in the
history of Rome has been placed more in the field of legend than in history.
The existence of the monarchical period was even questioned.



The historians of the nineteenth and much of the
twentieth century dismissed the tradition and history of the First kings of
Rome (Rómulo, Numa Pompilio, Tulio Hostilius) as well as the founding date,
(753 BC), and both were categorized as merely legendary.

The twentieth century has already been advanced
when, thanks to archaeology and other sciences, it has been able to revalorize
the Sustrayéndola tradition of many legendary elements and suspicious
interpretations.




Possibly, the first inhabitants of Rome came
from various parts and had neither the economic nor cultural development of
their northern neighbors, the Etruscans, or the south, the Sabines and Latinos.

In the  Palatine Archaeologists
found the remains of a primitive village of the 8th century B.C. with burials
at their feet. It seems to be, that from a nucleus the population was extended
by the slopes of the nearby hills and, in the following century, by the valley
between them.

EIFFEL TOWER



The Eiffel tower was built between the years 1887 and 1889 with the aim of
celebrating the world fair that commemorated the 100 years of the French
Revolution. It was opened to visitors finally on May 6th, 1889.

Gustave Eiffel was the one who designed the tower model. When its
construction was started many Parisians, especially the artists, did not like
the project and protested against it. For criticism, the tower would be but an
architectural uncertain. However, once built, the work became the most accepted
patrimony of the Gauls.

This tower was made of iron and weighs more than seven thousand tons. Every
seven years it is applied a coat of paint of a weight of 40 tons to avoid
oxidation. The building has a total height of 300 meters and a staircase with
1,666 steps. It is also equipped with original, but automated elevators, which
take visitors from the base to the top.

At the top of the tower there is a panoramic view of the city. Visitors,
many times, can feel the movement of the tower because of the air. However,
this does not affect any danger.

According to the story, the tower had the vision of being demolished for
the year of 1909. But thanks to the telegraphic antennas that were installed
and that served for the communication of that time, the dismantling was
avoided. From then on, the work is part of the French communication system,
since there are part antennas of radio and national television.


In itself, the Eiffel tower is considered the symbol of Paris and the most
recognized monument in all Europe. It is declared architecturally as a master
piece. By the year 2000 about 200 million people have already visited since
1889. And to increase the attractiveness, more than a decade ago, a night
lighting system was installed for the enjoyment of Parisians and visitors.

The tower of Pisa has a height of 55 meters, weighs 14,700 tons and has 300

As for the origins of the tower of Pisa, we find
ourselves facing a mystery... An inscription made on one of the walls of the
entrance of the tower says: "A.D. MCLXXIV. CAMPANILE HOC
FUIT FUNDATUM mense Augusti"That is to say, this belfry was
founded in the month of August, D.C. 1174. A curious fact is that in no record
of the time is written the name of the architect of the work.


This fact has been the subject of many debates still in force, as according
to the historian Aretine Giorgio Vasari, the architect of the tower is Bonanno
Pisano. But they were also recognized as possible authors to Diotisalvi,
(architect of the baptistery), and Bedouin, (the decoration of the tower is
similar to other works carried out by this architect). With all these
conjectures and hypotheses around the true creator of this great work, it seems
that still this name will remain in the mystery.
